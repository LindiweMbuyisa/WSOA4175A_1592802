Behind The Design

05 July 2021

By Lindiwe Mbuyisa

"Designer for creativity. Artist for accessibility and Creator for change."

Figuring out your niche as a creative is a difficult and challenging task to do. Furthermore creating a space for people to experience your work and have their perceived ideas is a difficult task to do. The representation becomes subjective to the users who will be looking through the website. The aim was to create a website for my body of work, showing the different work and beyond. It is a showcase of my work but also other interests as a designer. I decided to include projects that were reflective of the work I want to do. In that same breath I wanted to show the process and how it comes into play in each project I have done this by detailing the project in-depth. This website is not only an experience of the work but a combination of who I am, and what I define as.

But I wanted to acknowledge the design process and its importance when designing a website. It helps you have a clear idea and when going into the front end development, It makes it easier to code and tackle the development part of it. And so lets begin with the first thing I did which is finding inspiration. I often really try to get inspiration from mainly images and mediums outside of the website community. Looking at posters and image compositions really helps you design something that stands out and more you. The main thing was to look less website at how stunning are those award winning websites on CSS winner. Hence I used the inspiration and the personal branding project as the foundation for the website.

I begin all my designs with sketches and this helps me get into the flow of designing and trying out different layouts on paper where I can erase and just sketch without having to make everything perfect. The best part of the design process

The next step was jumping into Adobe XD and playing with different layouts and pushing the ideas/sketches I had further. I Slowly start incorporating the colours and typography. The aim was to try out different layouts and structure of the content on the website. Below are the different layouts to show how the idea progressed and evolved from the sketches to the final designs. Before getting to the final design, I also tried to play around with the typography and how I laid out the content on the website, further the colour combinations.

The final product

To explain the final design, I went with the big and bold typography taking up majority of the space on the website. With the interactions of the website, I wanted to use different interactions for links but also not taking away from ensuring that the user will not get confused by what which words are links and not. I used the comic boom shape as an overlay for the buttons. To ensure users are aware of the links; I have black outline with the hover creating depth by having it white if the background is a gradient. This is also applied to the outlines for the navigation of the website. This is to show in which part of the website the user is in. I have labelled in similar style.
