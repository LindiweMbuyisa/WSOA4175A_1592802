YOUTH DAY

June 16 also known as Youth Day is a commemoration of the uprising that began in Soweto and spread across South Africa which profoundly changed the socio-political landscape in South Africa. The events were triggered by the policies of the apartheid government that resulted in the introduction of the Bantu Education Act in 1953. The rise of the black consciousness movement and the formation of the South African students organisation raised the political consciousness of many students. While others joined the wave of the anti-apartheid sentiment within the student community when the language of Afrikaans and English were made compulsory as a medium of instruction in schools in 1974 and this led to black students mobilizing against such policies and rules of which resulted in the death of innocent young black students at the hands of police officers.
